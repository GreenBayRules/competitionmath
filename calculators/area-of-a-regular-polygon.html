Area of a regular polygon

The two formulas (s = side length, n = number of sides) are: `(s^2n)/(2tan(180/n))` or `(ap)/2`

The formula to this, in my opinion is very fascinating. There are a few steps though.

In short, the formula is `(ap)/2` where `a` is the apothem, and `p` is the perimeter.

But, what if you are only given the side length and the polygon name?

In that case, you need to get `a` in terms of `s` and `p` in terms of `s` and `n` (number of sides).

`p` is equal `sn` and `a` is equal to `s/(2tan(180/n))` You can look at the proof here

Combining that, gives you: `(s^2n)/(2tan(180/n))`
